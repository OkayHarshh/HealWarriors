Human Heart Overview

The human heart is a muscular organ about the size of a fist, located in the thoracic cavity between the lungs. It functions as a pump to circulate blood throughout the body via the circulatory system.

Key Characteristics:

  • Weight: 250-350 grams in adults
  • Dimensions: Approximately 12 cm long, 8 cm wide, and 6 cm thick
  • Location: Mediastinum, between the second and fifth intercostal spaces
  • Orientation: Apex points downward, forward, and to the left
  • Function: Pumps ~5 liters of blood per minute at rest (70 mL per beat)

Blood Circulation Pathway

The heart maintains a double circulatory system: pulmonary circulation (heart → lungs → heart) and systemic circulation (heart → body → heart). Oxygenated blood (red) is pumped to the body, while deoxygenated blood (blue) is sent to the lungs for reoxygenation.
